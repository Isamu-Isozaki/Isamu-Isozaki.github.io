Web development 0: First post!

Date:2018/06/02

The question that I will be addressing in the first post, naturally, is how will I structure this blog section. What I did for this first post is to make a row and insert the contents of this blog post into there. While this works fine, I think there is a certain degree of limitation when I try to say, post multiple blog posts. You, the users will need to scroll down! I am not especially being sarcastic here. I just find it to be quite an inconvenience to navigate through the blog posts and to remember the ones you liked.

What will I do then? There are multiple solutions to this. There are methods, such as putting all the blogs into a map or a newspaper format or making them rotate around on the home screen. I am not against either of these projects. In fact, it feels quite refreshing to be able to use what I have learnt in Udacity in a practical setting. But still, what will I do? Those two options I mentioned above sounds too flashy for my taste. Actually, having a carousel sounds quite intriguing. I think I will make a carousel on this site with all of my favorite blog posts and then add a nice looking search bar at the top to search for perticular blog posts. Then, I will have the particular blog posts under there in chronogical order. That sounds like a good start!

For the web developers out there, I will be using knockout.js to manage the data of the site and bootstrap for the stylish look. I will post any potential humorous incidents or mind bogglingly tiresome(debugging) incidents here. If I find something interesting, I will post that here as well!

To those of you who cannot care less about how I structure this blog, do not fear! There are other categories of blog posts other than web development, such as my struggles with chemistry and so on, and I plan to post them alongside my enjoyable experience as a web developer.

Now that I think about it, I will categorize my blog posts in a fun way in order to be visually appealing. I may need to use servers. I do not know almost anything about backend web developement so I may need to learn new content. However, I will reserve the pleasure for a later date when I am sure that that is the case!
